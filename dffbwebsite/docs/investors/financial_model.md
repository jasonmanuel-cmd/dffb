# Scalability Financial Model (5-Year Projections)

Detailed monthly and annual projections showing path to profitability and margin expansion.

---

## Key Assumptions (Adjust as needed)

### Unit Economics (Single Property)
- Average property: 8 beds
- Revenue per resident: $2,200/month (adjustable by region)
- Occupancy Year 1: 70%; Year 2: 80%; Year 3–5: 90%+
- Staffing ratio: 1:3 during day, 1:6 at night

### Operational Cost Assumptions (% of revenue)
- **Staffing:** Year 1: 70%, Year 2: 67%, Year 3+: 62% (efficiency improves with scale)
- **Utilities/food/supplies:** 12% (stable)
- **Licensing/insurance/compliance:** Year 1: 10%, Year 2–5: 7% (leverage)
- **Marketing/referral:** Year 1: 8%, Year 2–5: 3% (network effect)
- **Owner/landlord share:** 5% (if partnership model)

### Scaling Assumptions
- Add 2–3 properties per year (growth rate varies by scenario)
- Shared services (HR, compliance, marketing) reduce per-unit OpEx over time
- Occupancy improves as network matures and referral volume grows

## Key Insights

1. **Margin Expansion:** Gross margin fluctuates (due to ramp-up) but EBITDA margin improves from -6% to 25% by Year 5
2. **Scalability:** With only 40 properties and 320 beds, we're approaching $11M revenue and $2.8M EBITDA
3. **Operating Leverage:** Each additional property in Year 3+ adds ~$100K EBITDA due to shared services and network effects
4. **Occupancy Impact:** Every 1% occupancy improvement adds ~$25–50K EBITDA; network-driven occupancy growth is critical
5. **Cost Ratios:** Staffing cost (largest expense) improves from 70% in Year 1 to 62% by Year 3+ due to shared HR, training, recruitment

## Scenario Analysis

### Conservative Scenario (60% slower ramp)
- Properties: 1, 4, 8, 14, 20 (vs. 2, 8, 15, 28, 40)
- Year 5 EBITDA: ~$1.2M (vs. $2.8M base case)
- Still highly profitable; longer path to scale

### Aggressive Scenario (2x faster ramp)
- Properties: 3, 12, 25, 50, 80 (more capital required)
- Year 5 EBITDA: ~$6M+ (vs. $2.8M base case)
- Requires significant capital deployment and operational scaling

### Downside Scenario (occupancy 10% lower than assumed)
- Year 3 occupancy: 78% (vs. 88%)
- Year 5 EBITDA: ~$1.8M (vs. $2.8M base case)
- Still profitable; less upside

## Investor Returns (Illustrative)

- Exit valuation: $40M (5x Year 5 revenue)

- **Recurring cash: $420K/year; 5-year cumulative: $2.1M + equity upside**

- Exit valuation: $60M+ (5.5x revenue + growth premium)
- Investor ownership: 15%
- **Return: $9M+ on $2M = 4.5x+, 35%+ IRR**

## Notes for Investors

- **Model assumptions can be adjusted** based on your market, property size, and care model
- **Conservative on occupancy** — assumes 70% Year 1, 80% Year 2, 90%+ Year 3+
- **Staffing costs** are realistic for quality care homes; not cutting corners on staff
- **Capital deployment** assumes organic growth (not aggressive acquisition); can accelerate with more capital
- **Shared services leverage** is realistic — third-party RCFE operators often run 70%+ staffing; network leverage can bring this to 60–62%
