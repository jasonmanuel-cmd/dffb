# Investor Narrative — DFFB as Infrastructure Layer

## Core Narrative

DFFB is not an assisted-living operator. DFFB is a **senior housing infrastructure platform** that aggregates fragmented residential assets, operational expertise, and intake/placement networks into a scalable, recurring-revenue business.

### The Problem Being Solved

1. **Senior housing shortage:** 10,000+ seniors aging into care daily; 80% prefer aging in place (small homes, not institutions)
2. **Fragmented supply:** care is delivered by isolated mom-and-pop operators, understaffed nursing homes, and overpriced chains — no efficient middle market
3. **Broken referral system:** seniors/families struggle to find quality care; referral partners (hospitals, advisors) resort to institutional facilities by default
4. **Operator distress:** aging RCFE owners have no succession plan; burnout is high; occupancy is volatile

### The DFFB Model

DFFB operates as a **three-layer infrastructure platform:**

**Layer 1: Asset Aggregation**
- Acquire or lease distressed/inherited properties from families and operators
- Convert to licensed senior residential care (6–25 bed homes)
- Build a **networked portfolio of properties** across regions

**Layer 2: Operational Excellence**
- Deploy standardized systems: intake, staffing, compliance, marketing, occupancy management
- Replicate playbooks across all properties → economies of scale
- Reduce per-bed operational cost as scale increases

**Layer 3: Referral & Intake Network**
- Build a **centralized placement funnel** (hospitals, advisors, families, agencies)
- Direct high-intent seniors to DFFB properties
- Create **first-mover advantage in local markets** by being the "go-to" provider

### Why This Is Investable

1. **Recurring Revenue:** seniors stay 2–7 years; high lifetime value per resident
2. **Margin Improvement:** operational leverage increases margins from 12% (single property) to 20–30% at scale
3. **Moat:** network effects (more properties → more intake volume → better occupancy → more revenue to reinvest)
4. **Scalability:** proven model replicates across markets without founder involvement required
5. **Demographic Tailwind:** senior population growing 3–4%/year; demand >> supply

### Investment Horizon

- Years 1–2: Proof of concept (2–5 properties, validate unit economics)
- Years 2–4: Growth phase (expand to 15–30 properties across region)
- Years 4+: Mature network (dominant market position, 50+ properties, recurring revenue base)
- Exit optionality: acquisition by larger care provider, roll-up platform, or hold for cash flow
