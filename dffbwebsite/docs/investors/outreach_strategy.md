# Investor Outreach Strategy

Multi-channel, segmented approach to reaching and converting investors.

---

## Outreach Phases

### Phase 1: Pre-Launch (Months 1–2)
**Goal:** Warm intros, soft signaling, advisor recruitment

**Activities:**
- Identify 20–30 potential advisors (entrepreneurs, operators, healthcare folks with networks)
- Request 15-min intro calls: "we're building senior housing infrastructure; want strategic input"
- Share vision one-pager (NOT a pitch deck) to gather feedback
- Recruit 2–3 advisors to join advisory board (unpaid; equity incentive)
- Build cap table and term sheet draft with counsel

**Outputs:**
- 2–3 advisors committed
- Feedback loop on messaging (what resonates, what doesn't)
- Warm intro pathways into investor networks

---

### Phase 2: Soft Launch (Months 2–4)
**Goal:** Build credibility assets, pilot property data, investor conversations

**Activities:**
- Complete first property conversion and document results
- Capture metrics: occupancy, resident satisfaction, financials
- Create case study / testimonial from first landlord/operator
- 1:1 conversations with 15–20 warm-intro investors (coffee/video calls, 30–45 min)
- Refine pitch based on investor feedback
- Build investors list (segmented by type and check size)

**Outputs:**
- First property case study
- Refined pitch narrative
- 15–20 warm relationship conversations
- List of "likely" investors (30+)

---

### Phase 3: Active Raise (Months 4–9)
**Goal:** Convert investors to commitments

**Activities:**
- Formal pitch meetings with target investors (30 min preso + Q&A)
- Property tours for serious prospects
- Reference calls with landlords, residents, staff
- Term sheet negotiation with lead investors
- Board formation with lead investor

**Outputs:**
- Target: $[X]M committed (mix of lead, follow-on investors)
- 2–3 term sheets in negotiation
- 1 lead investor identified
- Legal documents drafted

---

### Phase 4: Close & Deploy (Months 9–12)
**Goal:** Capital close and begin deployment

**Activities:**
- Final legal documentation and fund administration
- Capital deployment begins (property acquisitions, team building)
- Monthly investor updates commence
- Quarterly reporting and board meetings

---

## Outreach Channels (Prioritized)

### Channel 1: Warm Intros (Highest ROI)
**Timeline:** Months 1–12 (ongoing)
**Strategy:** Leverage existing networks to get intros to investors

**How to execute:**
- List all founders, advisors, operators you know with investor networks
- Request warm intro: "I'm working with [Name] on senior housing. Would you intro me to 1–2 investors you know in [healthcare/real estate/impact]?"
- Goal: 2–3 intros per week = 50–80 warm intros over 6 months

**Expected conversion:** 20–30% of warm intros → coffee calls; 10–20% of coffee calls → pitch meetings; 30–50% of pitch meetings → investor interest

---

### Channel 2: Direct Outreach to Target Investors
**Timeline:** Months 2–9
**Strategy:** LinkedIn, email, cold calls to investor targets

**List sources:**
- Crunchbase (filter: healthcare funds, senior care investments, real estate funds)
- PitchBook (same filters)
- AngelList (healthcare, impact investor networks)
- Local angel groups and investor networks
- Industry directories (LeadingAge, PERE, healthcare events)

**Outreach template:**

**Email subject:** "Senior housing infrastructure opportunity — 3-min question"

**Body:**
"Hi [Name], I'm [Your Name] — building a platform to consolidate fragmented senior housing. We're solving a $150B market with structural supply gaps. Wondering if senior care / real estate infrastructure interests you? Happy to share a one-pager or hop on a quick call if relevant.

[Link to one-pager or deck]
[Your phone]"

**Expected conversion:** 3–5% response rate; 20% of respondents → calls; 30–50% of calls → pitch meetings

---

### Channel 3: Events & Speaking
**Timeline:** Months 2–12
**Strategy:** Position DFFB at conferences, meetups, panel discussions

**Event types:**
- Healthcare investing conferences (LeadingAge, MedCity News events)
- Real estate investor groups and conferences
- Impact investing forums (DPI, GIIN, local impact groups)
- Angel networks and pitch competitions
- Senior living / geriatric care professional groups

**Activities at events:**
- Sponsor or speak on "consolidation in senior housing" or "demographic megatrends"
- Host breakfast / roundtable for investors interested in topic
- Pitch competition (if applicable)
- Informal networking (booth, happy hour, breakout rooms)

**Expected conversion:** 2–3 quality leads per event; aim for 3–4 events in 6 months = 6–12 warm leads

---

### Channel 4: Inbound (Content + PR)
**Timeline:** Months 1–12 (build over time)
**Strategy:** Inbound interest from thought leadership

**Activities:**
- Blog/article: "Why Senior Housing Is Broken (And How to Fix It)" on Medium, LinkedIn, healthcare publications
- Podcast appearances (healthcare, impact investing podcasts)
- Forbes / TechCrunch / VentureBeat pitch (if newsworthy)
- Industry publication features (Senior Housing News, etc.)
- PR outreach highlighting pilot results

**Expected conversion:** 1–2 inbound inquiries per month after 3 months = 6–12 inbound leads over 6 months

## Investor Segmentation & Customized Outreach

### Segment A: Healthcare Funds
**Outreach message:** "We're building consolidation infrastructure in senior housing—like Wellio or Parsley Health but for RCFE market."
**Value prop:** Fragmented market, consolidation opportunity, recurring revenue
**Meeting length:** 45 min + property tour
**Ask:** $2–10M, board seat or advisory role
**Timeline to close:** 2–3 months

### Segment B: Family Offices
**Outreach message:** "Boring, recurring-revenue business solving demographic megatrend."
**Value prop:** Patient capital, long-term hold, high reliability
**Meeting length:** 30–45 min
**Ask:** $1–5M, limited governance
**Timeline to close:** 3–4 months

### Segment C: Real Estate Funds
**Outreach message:** "Stable cash-flowing assets with operational upside as properties scale."
**Value prop:** Asset appreciation, occupancy stability, long-term hold
**Meeting length:** 45 min + financials deep dive
**Ask:** $1–5M (often co-invest with operations partner)
**Timeline to close:** 2–3 months

### Segment D: Impact Investors
**Outreach message:** "Addressing housing shortage for seniors + creating quality jobs + measurable outcomes."
**Value prop:** Impact + returns
**Meeting length:** 45 min + impact metrics
**Ask:** $500K–$3M
**Timeline to close:** 3–4 months

### Segment E: Local Angels
**Outreach message:** "Help us solve senior housing crisis in [Region]. Be an investor + advisor."
**Value prop:** Community impact, local network, advisory participation
**Meeting length:** 30–45 min
**Ask:** $100K–$1M
**Timeline to close:** 1–2 months

## Pitch Meeting Agenda (45 min)

**0–5 min:** Warm-up, context-setting
**5–15 min:** Problem + opportunity (why now?)
**15–25 min:** Solution + unit economics (how DFFB wins)
**25–30 min:** Traction + team (why bet on us?)
**30–40 min:** Financial model + ask (what we need)
**40–45 min:** Q&A + next steps

**Materials to have on hand:**
- Pitch deck (digital + printed)
- One-pager summary
- Financial model (copy)
- Property case study
- Team bios
- Cap table draft
- Appendix (market data, comparable companies, risk analysis)

---

## Post-Meeting Follow-Up

**Within 24 hours:**
- Thank you email with promised materials
- Recap of conversation and investor's interest level
- Next step / timeline suggestion

**Within 1 week:**
- Reference calls (landlord, operator, advisor)
- Property tour invitation (if interested)
- Financial model deep-dive (if requested)

**Ongoing (every 2–4 weeks):**
- Progress updates (new property, occupancy improvements, team hires)
- Investor newsletter (monthly or quarterly)
- Term sheet negotiation

**Breakdown:**

**Investor composition:**
- 1 lead healthcare or family office fund
- 2–3 follow-on investors (mix of types)
- 5–10 smaller angels or regional investors

---

## Red Flags to Avoid

**Investors to deprioritize:**
- Those who want immediate exits (this is a 5–7 year hold)
- Those asking for >50% board control (overly hands-on)
- Those with unrealistic return expectations (this is 25–35% IRR, not 10x)
- Those unfamiliar with senior care / recurring revenue models
- Those without follow-on capital (one-check wonders)
- Those with poor terms (excessive dilution, onerous governance)

---

## Success Metrics

- **Raise goal achieved:** $[X]M by Month 6–9
- **Investor quality:** Mix of strategic + financial investors; strong networks
- **Board strength:** 1–2 lead investors + 1–2 independent advisors
- **Pipeline health:** 2–3 properties operational, 90%+ occupancy, $[X] revenue, EBITDA positive
- **Team in place:** CEO + COO + CFO + Operations manager
- **Momentum:** Monthly property additions, increasing occupancy, positive testimonials
