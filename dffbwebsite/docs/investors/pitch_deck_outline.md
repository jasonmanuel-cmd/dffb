# Investor Pitch Deck Outline (12–15 slides)

Simple structure for a compelling, outcome-focused investor pitch.

---

## Slide 1: Cover
**Title:** "Senior Housing Infrastructure"
**Subtitle:** "Solving fragmentation at scale"
**Tagline:** "Building a network-enabled, recurring-revenue platform"

*Visual: clean, professional; map showing target markets or before/after property images*

---

## Slide 2: The Problem (1 visual)
**Headline:** "Senior housing is fragmented, fragile, and in crisis"

**Key stats:**
- 10,000+ seniors aging into care daily
- 65+ population growing 3–4%/year
- 80% prefer aging in place (small homes, not institutions)
- **REALITY:** no dominant provider, 60% of supply is understaffed mom-and-pop RCFEs, referral system is broken

*Visual: fragmented pie chart, trend line showing population growth, supply gap*

---

## Slide 3: The Opportunity (1 visual)
**Headline:** "$150B+ fragmented market ripe for infrastructure consolidation"

**Key insight:**
- Market is 10x larger than most people realize
- Only 30–40% of supply meets seniors' preference (home-like, quality)
- Consolidation opportunity: top 5 operators control <20% of market
- First-mover advantage: build the platform, own the market

*Visual: TAM breakdown, market growth curve, competitive landscape*

---

## Slide 4: Why It's Solvable (Strategy)
**Headline:** "Three-layer platform solves all three problems"

**Layer 1: Assets** — aggregate distressed/inherited properties
**Layer 2: Operations** — deploy standardized systems
**Layer 3: Network** — build centralized intake funnel

*Visual: simple diagram showing three layers, feedback loops*

---

## Slide 5: Unit Economics (The Hook)
**Headline:** "Boring, reliable, recurring revenue"

**Key metrics:**
- 8-bed facility at 90% occupancy: $190K/year revenue
- Operating margin at scale: 20–25%
- Resident lifetime value: $150K–$200K
- Occupancy improves with network (75% → 95% over 3 years)

*Visual: simple waterfall or bar chart showing revenue → costs → margin progression*

---

## Slide 6: Competitive Advantage
**Headline:** "Network effects create a defensible moat"

**Why DFFB wins:**
- More properties → better intake consistency → better occupancy
- Standardized operations → margins improve with scale
- Brand + referral relationships → first-mover advantage in market
- Data advantage → continuous optimization

*Visual: flywheel or network diagram showing reinforcing loops*

---

## Slide 7: Proof of Concept (or Early Traction)
**Headline:** "We've validated the model"

**Metrics from pilot properties:**
- Property 1: [X beds, Y% occupancy, $Z/month revenue]
- Property 2: [X beds, Y% occupancy, $Z/month revenue]
- Resident satisfaction: 90%+
- Staff retention: 85%+
- Referral partner count: X active

*Visual: metrics dashboard or simple property cards with numbers*

---

## Slide 8: Growth Plan (5-Year)
**Headline:** "Scaling from pilot to market leader"

**Roadmap:**
- Year 1: 2–3 properties (proof of concept)
- Year 2: 8–12 properties (local market dominance)
- Year 3: 20–25 properties (regional leader)
- Year 4–5: 40–50 properties (market-leading platform)

*Visual: geographic map showing expansion, property count growth curve*

---

## Slide 9: Financial Projections
**Headline:** "Clear path to profitability and scale"

| Year | Properties | Revenue | EBITDA | Margin |
|------|-----------|---------|--------|--------|
| 1 | 2 | $285K | $34K | 12% |
| 2 | 8 | $1.4M | $224K | 16% |
| 3 | 15 | $3.2M | $640K | 20% |
| 5 | 40 | $11.2M | $2.8M | 25% |

*Visual: stacked bar chart (revenue + EBITDA), or simple line graphs*

---

## Slide 10: Risk Mitigation
**Headline:** "We've thought through the risks"

**Key risks + mitigations:**
- Regulatory: Full licensing + legal review + insurance
- Occupancy: Diverse intake + competitive pricing + high quality
- Staffing: Retention bonuses + shared recruitment + competitive comp
- Market: Demographic tailwind + structural supply gap + long-term megatrend

*Visual: simple risk matrix (low-impact / low-likelihood items not listed)*

---

## Slide 11: Investment Ask
**Headline:** "We're raising $[X]M to scale to [target cities/properties]"

**Use of capital:**
- 50% → acquisitions & conversions (deposits, initial staffing, renovations)
- 25% → operations team (CFO, ops manager, compliance)
- 15% → tech & systems (intake platform, reporting)
- 10% → marketing & partnerships

*Visual: pie chart or simple bars showing allocation*

---

## Slide 12: Exit & Returns
**Headline:** "Multiple paths to investor returns"

**Scenarios:**
- Acquisition by larger senior care operator (acquisition premium)
- Roll-up platform sale (platform multiples)
- Hold for recurring cash flow (dividend or buyout)

**Target returns:** 25–35% IRR over 5–7 years

*Visual: simple exit timeline or return waterfall*

---

## Slide 13: Team
**Headline:** "Experienced team with proven execution"

**Bios (short):**
- Founder/CEO: [background in senior care / operations / fundraising]
- COO: [operational expertise, track record]
- CFO/Finance: [financial background, unit economics expertise]
- Advisors: [credible, relevant advisors from senior care, real estate, healthcare]

*Visual: team photos, logos of previous companies/accomplishments*

---

## Slide 14: Call to Action
**Headline:** "Join us in building senior housing infrastructure"

**Next steps:**
- Schedule 30-min deep dive
- Site visit to operational property
- Reference calls with pilot operators/landlords

**Contact:** [Email, phone, calendar link]

*Visual: simple, clean contact info; maybe a provocative stat or quote*

---

## Slide 15: Appendix (optional, speaker notes)
- Detailed financial model (monthly projections)
- Regulatory overview (licensing, compliance)
- Comparable company multiples
- Detailed risk analysis
- Market data and trends

---

## Design Notes

- **Style:** Clean, professional, data-driven (not flashy or gimmicky)
- **Colors:** Trust-building palette (blues, greens, whites)
- **Visuals:** Real property photos, resident/staff images (authentic, not stock)
- **Typography:** Large, readable; whitespace important
- **Tone:** Confident but humble; outcome-focused, not hype-focused

---

**Slide 1–2:** "We're solving a crisis. 10,000 seniors a day need housing. Current system is broken."
**Slide 3:** "But this crisis is also an opportunity—$150B market, mostly unconsolidated."
**Slide 4:** "We're not just running homes; we're building infrastructure—platform, not point solution."
**Slide 5:** "Here's why the math works: proven unit economics, margin expansion with scale."
**Slide 6:** "Here's why we'll win: network effects create defensibility."
**Slide 7:** "We've already proven this works—here's our pilot data."
**Slide 8–9:** "Here's how we scale and what the returns look like."
**Slide 10:** "We've thought carefully about risks and have mitigations in place."
**Slide 11–12:** "We're raising capital to execute this plan. Here's how you make your money back."
**Slide 13:** "We have a team that can execute this—here's who they are."
**Slide 14:** "Let's have a conversation about joining us."

---

## Customization Tips

- **For healthcare funds:** emphasize consolidation opportunity, operational playbooks, exit to larger operator
- **For family offices:** emphasize recurring revenue, long-term hold, demographic tailwind
- **For real estate funds:** emphasize asset appreciation, stable cash flow, property value creation
- **For impact investors:** emphasize social impact metrics, community benefit, measurable outcomes
- **For operators/strategic:** emphasize partnership potential, co-investment structure, playbook licensing
