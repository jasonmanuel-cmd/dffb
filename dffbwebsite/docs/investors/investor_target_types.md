# Investor Target Types

Segmented investor profile for outreach and customization.

---

## 1. Family Offices ($100M–$5B AUM)

### Profile
- Patient capital, 5–10 year horizon
- Prefer recurring revenue and "boring" business (lower risk profile)
- Strong network effects matter to them
- Multi-check ability (can follow on in later rounds)

### Why They're Interested in DFFB
- Demographic tailwind is generational
- Recurring revenue businesses command premium multiples
- Management team execution is testable
- Clear path to scale without capital-intensity

### Messaging Angle
"This is a long-term, recurring-revenue business solving a structural market problem. Low hype, high reliability. A decade-long hold."

### Outreach Channels
- Warm intros through existing advisors/networks
- Family office conferences
- Direct outreach to COOs/heads of operations

### Typical Check Size
$1M–$5M

---

## 2. Healthcare-Focused Funds ($500M–$5B AUM)

### Profile
- Deep expertise in senior care, health IT, services
- Understand unit economics and operator challenges
- Can add strategic value (operational expertise, vendor relationships)
- Looking for "add-on" acquisition platforms

### Why They're Interested in DFFB
- Fragmented market with consolidation opportunity
- Proprietary operational playbooks
- Recurring senior care revenue is recession-resistant
- Exit potential (roll-up to larger operator or PE hold)

### Messaging Angle
"We're building the platform that consolidates the fragmented RCFE market. You bring scale; we bring operational excellence."

### Outreach Channels
- Healthcare conferences (HIMSS, LeadingAge, etc.)
- Warm intros from healthcare VCs
- LinkedIn / direct outreach to healthcare fund partners

### Typical Check Size
$2M–$10M

---

## 3. Real Estate / Property Funds

### Profile
- Asset-focused (care about property value, NOI, appreciation)
- Prefer long-term hold models
- Understand leverage and refinancing
- May partner on capital stack (debt + equity)

### Why They're Interested in DFFB
- Properties appreciate under good management
- Long-term tenant (senior residents) = stable cash flow
- Diversification from commercial real estate
- Demographic demand is predictable

### Messaging Angle
"Every property we convert becomes a stabilized, cash-flowing asset. You own the real estate; we manage the operations and occupancy."

### Outreach Channels
- Real estate investor groups and conferences
- PERE (Private Equity Real Estate) forums
- Direct relationships with CRE funds

### Typical Check Size
$1M–$5M (often co-invested with operations partner)

---

## 4. Local/Regional Investors & Angels

### Profile
- Local market knowledge and networks
- Smaller check sizes but high follow-on potential
- Often place value on community impact
- May serve on advisory board

### Why They're Interested in DFFB
- Can see the problem directly in their community
- Network of referral partners (hospitals, advisors, advisors)
- Opportunity to co-invest and support local ecosystem
- Personal interest in senior care (aging parents, demographics)

### Messaging Angle
"Help us solve the senior housing shortage in [Region]. We're hiring investors who can also serve as advisors and referral partners."

### Outreach Channels
- Local business groups and chambers
- Angel networks and accelerators
- Dinner series and community events
- Direct outreach to prominent local entrepreneurs/retirees

### Typical Check Size
$100K–$1M

---

## 5. Venture Capital (if scaling rapidly)

### Profile
- Faster capital, growth-focused
- May push for faster expansion / revenue growth
- Prefer founder-led companies
- Looking for 10x+ returns in 5–7 years

### Why They're Interested in DFFB
- Large TAM ($150B+)
- Software-like scaling potential (playbook + capital)
- Network effects = defensible business
- Path to unicorn valuation if you reframe as "platform" not "operator"

### Messaging Angle
"We're building a software-like operating system for senior housing. Scale is limited only by capital, not complexity."

### Outreach Channels
- VC pitch competitions
- Warm intros from successful founders in adjacent spaces
- LinkedIn outreach to healthcare-focused VCs
- Demo days and pitch events

### Typical Check Size
$2M–$10M

---

## 6. Impact Investors

### Profile
- Return + social impact are equally important
- Patient capital (10+ year horizon)
- Often have deep connections to nonprofits, foundations
- Strong values alignment matters

### Why They're Interested in DFFB
- Solving housing shortage for underserved population
- Creating jobs and community impact
- Demographic justice angle (elders deserve quality care)
- Measurable outcomes (occupancy, resident satisfaction, staff retention)

### Messaging Angle
"This is social impact that also returns strong financial results. We're solving a crisis while building a profitable business."

### Outreach Channels
- Impact investor networks (DPI, GIIN, Confluence)
- ESG-focused fund managers
- Foundations and family office sustainability teams
- Impact conferences

### Typical Check Size
$500K–$3M

---

## 7. Operator/Strategic Partners (Existing Senior Care Providers)

### Profile
- Already in the market; understand operators' pain
- May acquire as "tuck-in" or co-invest
- Bring operational expertise, vendor relationships, referral networks
- Can move quickly on deployment

### Why They're Interested in DFFB
- Fragmentation consolidation opportunity
- DFFB model solves problems they face (occupancy, staffing)
- Can acquire properties and deploy DFFB playbook
- Defensible market position

### Messaging Angle
"Let's partner to scale your model across a fragmented market. We'll handle operations; you bring credibility and referrals."

### Outreach Channels
- Industry conferences (LeadingAge, CCAL)
- Direct outreach to COOs of larger senior care chains
- Trade publications and industry events
- M&A advisors focused on senior care

### Typical Check Size
Variable (acquisition or co-investment; larger check sizes possible)

---

## Outreach Priority Ranking

**Quick Wins (3–6 months):**
1. Local/regional angels + family offices with healthcare interest
2. Existing senior care operators (strategic partners)

**Medium-term (6–12 months):**
3. Healthcare-focused funds
4. Real estate funds with senior housing interest

**Longer-term (12+ months):**
5. Larger VC funds (once proof of concept is very clear)
6. Impact investors (easier to raise from once you have traction)

---

## Customization Template

For each investor type, adapt your pitch:

1. **Opening:** Frame problem in their language (demographic for healthcare; asset appreciation for RE; impact for impact funds)
2. **Unit Economics:** Emphasize what they care about (recurring revenue, occupancy, margins, appreciation)
3. **Competitive Advantage:** Highlight defensibility (network effects, operational playbooks, market position)
4. **Use of Capital:** Specify how you'll deploy their capital (properties, team, tech, partners)
5. **Exit Path:** Clear path to return (acquisition, roll-up, hold for cash flow, etc.)
