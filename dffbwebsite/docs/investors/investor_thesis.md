## The Market Problem

### Size & Growth
- **TAM:** $150B+ senior care market (residential, assisted living, memory care, skilled nursing)
- **Growth:** 65+ population growing 3–4%/year; care demand even higher
- **Preference:** 80% of seniors prefer aging in place (home-like environment) vs. institutions
- **Supply gap:** only 30–40% of supply meets this preference; rest is institutional or unaffordable

### The Fragmentation Problem
- **No dominant player:** top 5 operators control <20% of market
- **Mom-and-pop RCFEs:** 60%+ are small (6–25 beds), independent, understaffed, no standards
- **No referral efficiency:** seniors/families have no clear way to find quality care; referral partners resort to large, impersonal chains
- **Operator distress:** average RCFE owner age 70+; 70% have no succession plan; burnout is endemic

### The Opportunity
A **networked, standardized platform** can:
- Aggregate fragmented properties into a coherent asset base
- Deploy professional management and systems across properties
- Build a **centralized intake funnel** that captures high-intent demand
- Achieve margin expansion and occupancy improvement through scale and efficiency

## DFFB's Solution

### Business Model

**Three-layer platform:**

1. **Asset Layer:** Acquire/lease distressed properties; convert to licensed residential care homes
2. **Operations Layer:** Deploy standardized systems (staffing, compliance, marketing, occupancy management)
3. **Network Layer:** Build centralized referral funnel (hospitals, advisors, agencies) → direct placement to DFFB homes

### Unit Economics (Single Property, Steady State)

**Assumptions:**
- 8-bed facility
- $2,200/resident/month average revenue
- 90% occupancy

**Revenue:**
- 8 beds × $2,200 × 90% × 12 months = $190,080/year

**Operating Costs (% of revenue):**
- Staffing: 65% = $123,552
- Utilities, food, supplies: 12% = $22,810
- Licensing, insurance, compliance: 8% = $15,206
- Owner/landlord share (if partnership): 5% = $9,504
- **Total OpEx: 90% = $171,072**

**Gross Margin:** 10% = $19,008/year per facility

**With Network & Scale Effects:**
- Occupancy improves to 95% (network provides consistent intake)
- Staffing efficiency improves to 60% (shared services, training, recruitment)
- Reductions in per-unit admin/compliance (shared legal, licensing)
- **Adjusted margin: 20–25% = $40,000–$47,500/year per facility**

## Competitive Advantage

### Network Effects
- More properties → more intake diversity → more consistent occupancy
- Better occupancy → higher revenue per bed → more reinvestment capacity
- Larger network → more bargaining power with referral partners and vendors

### Operational Moat
- Standardized playbooks across all properties
- Shared services (HR, compliance, marketing) reduce per-unit cost
- Continuous improvement culture embedded in operations

### Market Position
- First-mover advantage in select markets
- Brand recognition among referral partners (hospitals, advisors, agencies)
- Data advantage: insights into referral patterns, occupancy trends, resident outcomes

## Risk Mitigation

### Regulatory Risk
- Fully licensed facilities; proactive compliance
- Legal review of all operational procedures
- Insurance coverage for resident care and property liability

### Occupancy Risk
- Diverse intake funnel reduces dependence on single source
- Competitive pricing and high care quality drive retention
- Conservative occupancy assumptions (80–90% vs. 95%+)

### Staffing Risk
- Retention bonuses and staff development programs
- Competitive salaries and benefits
- Shared recruitment and training infrastructure

### Market/Demand Risk
- Demographic tailwind (65+ growing 3–4%/year)
- Shortage of quality care (structural supply gap)
- Long-term aging megatrend, not cyclical
