# Minimum Investment Structures

Clear, flexible investment options for different investor types and check sizes.

---

## Structure 1: Priced Equity Round (Series A)

### For: Institutional investors (healthcare funds, family offices, larger VCs)

**Investment:**
- Minimum: $500K–$1M
- Typical: $2M–$5M
- Maximum: $10M+ (larger funds)

**Terms:**
- Equity stake: Determined by valuation
- Liquidation preference: 1x non-participating preferred (or negotiated)
- Anti-dilution: Standard weighted-average
- Board seat: For investors >$2M
- Information rights: Monthly financials, quarterly board meetings

**Governance:**
- Board: 3–5 members (founder + lead investor + 1–2 independent)
- Reserved matters: budget >$100K, new hires >$200K salary, new market entry, asset dispositions
- Voting: 50%+ investor approval required for reserved matters

**Exit scenarios:**
- Acquisition by larger senior care operator (5–8 year horizon)
- Roll-up to platform (6–10 year horizon)
- Hold for recurring cash flow (dividend model, 7+ years)

**Expected IRR:** 25–35% over 5–7 years

## Structure 2: SAFE / Y Combinator Instrument

### For: Early investors (angels, smaller VCs, accelerators)

**Investment:**
- Minimum: $50K–$100K
- Typical: $250K–$750K
- Maximum: $1M

**Terms:**
- SAFE note (Simple Agreement for Future Equity)
- Valuation cap: $[X]M
- Discount: 10–20% (for early risk)
- Converts to equity on priced round or exit

**Governance:**
- No board seat
- Quarterly updates (email + metrics dashboard)
- MFN (Most Favored Nation) clause: if earlier investor got better terms, you get those terms
- Pro-rata rights: right to maintain ownership % in future rounds

**Advantages:**
- Fast documentation (1–2 weeks vs. 4–6 weeks for equity round)
- Lower legal costs
- Founder retains control

**Expected IRR:** 25–40% over 5–7 years (slightly higher due to early-stage risk premium)

## Structure 3: Convertible Note

### For: Bridge funding or strategic investors

**Investment:**
- Minimum: $100K
- Typical: $250K–$1M
- Maximum: $3M

**Terms:**
- Interest rate: 5–8% annual
- Maturity: 18–24 months
- Conversion on Series A priced round
- Valuation cap: $[X]M
- Discount: 10–20%

**Governance:**
- Advisory seat or quarterly call (if >$250K)
- Financial reporting: quarterly updates
- Maturity exit: if no Series A, converts to equity or negotiates extension

**Advantages:**
- Fast close (2–3 weeks)
- Lower legal costs than equity
- Aligns early investors with Series A success

**Expected IRR:** 25–40% (depending on Series A valuation and interest accrual)

## Structure 4: Equity as Part of Property/Lease Deal

### For: Real estate investors, landlords, operators

**Investment:**
- Equity stake in single property or portfolio
- Structures vary by deal

**Common approach:**
- Landlord retains 20–30% ownership
- DFFB operates and manages
- Profits split per agreed waterfall
- Landlord has put/call option at certain milestones or years

**Governance:**
- Annual meeting with landlord
- Quarterly P&L reports for the property
- Major decisions (capital improvements >$50K) require landlord approval
- Clear exit mechanics (DFFB buyout, third-party sale, refinance)

**Example deal:**
- Landlord contributes property valued at $400K
- DFFB contributes $100K in improvements + operations
- Ownership: 75% landlord / 25% DFFB operator
- Revenue split: 70% landlord / 30% DFFB (or fixed rent + profit share)

## Structure 5: Revenue Share / Royalty

### For: Strategic partners, patient capital

**Investment:**
- Minimum: $500K–$1M

**Terms:**
- Fixed % of annual revenue (not equity)
- Paid quarterly or annually
- Duration: 5–10 years or until 2–3x return achieved
- No board seat
- Limited governance rights

**Example:**
- Investor puts in $1M
- Receives 5% of annual DFFB revenue
- Year 1: $1.38M revenue × 5% = $69K paid to investor
- Year 3: $3.16M revenue × 5% = $158K paid to investor
- Investor receives $500K+ over 5 years, then royalty can end or convert to equity

**Advantages:**
- Simple to understand
- Aligns incentives (investor benefits from revenue growth)
- Lower dilution for founder
- Predictable cash return

## Structure 6: Debt + Equity Hybrid

### For: Real estate/infrastructure funds

**Investment:**
- Minimum: $1M–$2M

**Terms:**
- Secured debt: $[X] at 6–8% interest (secured by property)
- Equity kicker: % equity in operating company (5–15%)
- Term: 5–7 years
- Maturity: Balloon payment or refinance at maturity

**Governance:**
- Board seat (for larger check)
- Quarterly reporting
- Debt covenants: occupancy >75%, DBITDA coverage >1.5x
- Equity upside on exit

**Example:**
- Investor puts $1.5M
- $1M is senior debt at 7%
- $500K is equity (10% stake)
- Exit: property sold for $6M; investor gets $1M debt repay + 10% equity returns ($500K)

## Structure 7: Fund / SPV (Special Purpose Vehicle)

### For: Multiple investors, pooled approach

**Investment:**
- Minimum: Any ($50K–$5M per investor)
- Total fund size: $[X]M

**Terms:**
- Creates LLC / Fund for DFFB investment
- Fund invests as single entity
- Each investor is member of fund (with % ownership)
- Fund terms: equity stake, board representation, governance

**Advantages:**
- Simplifies administration (one investor for DFFB)
- Enables smaller checks ($50K–$250K)
- Syndication efficiency (fund manager handles governance)
- Tax efficiency (flow-through structure)

**Typical fund structure:**
- Fund manager: 2–3% annual management fee
- Carried interest: 20% of profit (fund manager gets 20% of upside)
- Investor commitment: capital + follow-on reserves
- Term: 10 years (or until exit)

## Recommendation by Investor Type

**Healthcare Fund (>$500M AUM, $2–5M check):**
→ Priced Equity Round + Board Seat

**Family Office ($100M–$1B AUM, $1–5M check):**
→ Priced Equity Round or SAFE (if early) + Advisory seat

**Real Estate Fund ($500M–$5B AUM, $1–3M check):**
→ Debt + Equity Hybrid or Equity in Property Deal

**Local Angel ($100K–$1M check):**
→ SAFE or Convertible Note + Pro-rata rights

**Strategic Partner / Operator ($500K–$2M check):**
→ Revenue Share or Equity in Property

**Impact Investor ($500K–$3M check):**
→ Priced Equity (if mission-aligned) or Revenue Share

---

## Key Negotiation Points

1. **Liquidation preference:** Non-participating is better for founder (investor gets their money back, no double-dip); participating is better for investor
2. **Anti-dilution:** Weighted-average is founder-friendly; full ratchet is investor-friendly
3. **Board seat:** Don't give away too easily; retain control for founder
4. **Information rights:** Balance transparency (good) with reporting burden (bad)
5. **Pro-rata rights:** Allow up to 2–3 lead investors; don't over-commit to secondary follow-ons
6. **Reserved matters:** Keep threshold high ($250K+) to retain operating flexibility

## Legal Documentation Timeline

- **Equity round:** 4–6 weeks (complex docs, multiple rounds of negotiation)
- **SAFE:** 1–2 weeks (template available, minimal negotiation)
- **Convertible:** 2–3 weeks (moderate negotiation)
- **MOU/LOI:** 1 week (just a handshake; finalizes economics before legal)

**Pro tip:** Have a standard MOU template ready for investors. Finalizes key terms (amount, valuation, structure) before expensive legal docs are drafted.
