# Investor Credibility Assets — What to Build & Show

Materials that build trust and demonstrate execution capability.

---

## Asset 1: Pilot Property Case Study

**What:** Detailed write-up of first 1–2 properties converted, with real metrics.

**Include:**
- Property background: address, size, condition before conversion, initial owner situation
- Conversion process: timeline, cost, renovations, licensing
- Operational metrics: Month 1–6 (occupancy %, revenue, staffing, resident count)
- Resident stories: 2–3 short testimonials from residents/families
- Occupancy ramp: chart showing how occupancy grew over time
- Financial results: actual P&L vs. projections
- Lessons learned: what worked, what didn't
- Owner/operator feedback: would they recommend? Why?

**Format:** 4–6 page PDF + visual dashboard

**Timing:** Ready by Month 3–4 (after property has operated for 3+ months)

---

## Asset 2: Landlord/Operator Testimonials

**What:** Video or written endorsements from satisfied property owners or operators you've worked with.

**Format:**
- 1–2 minute video testimonials (authentic, not scripted)
- Written quotes (2–3 sentences each)
- Include: name, property, quote, contact info (optional)

**Examples:**
- "They turned my vacant property into a $[X]/month revenue stream. Hands-off, professional, exactly what I needed." — Jane Owner, [City]
- "In 4 months, they stabilized occupancy from 65% to 88%. My staff is happier, residents are well cared for, and the business is finally sustainable." — Bob Operator, [Facility]

**Timing:** Build as you close first 2–3 deals

---

## Asset 3: Financial Model & Unit Economics Proof

**What:** Actual financial data from pilot properties showing your projections vs. reality.

**Include:**
- Monthly P&L from first property (anonymized or real)
- Actual occupancy vs. projected
- Actual revenue vs. projected
- Actual cost ratios vs. projected
- Variance analysis (why differences?)
- Adjusted projections for future properties based on learnings

**Format:** Excel model + summary dashboard

**Timing:** Quarterly updates as new data comes in

---

## Asset 4: Advisor / Board Credibility

**What:** Strong board and advisors lend credibility to investor conversations.

**Who to recruit:**
- Successful healthcare operator or hospital executive
- Real estate investor with track record
- Healthcare fund partner or PE investor
- Probate attorney or estate planner (referral source)
- Senior care industry expert or researcher

**Ask:** Advisory board position (equity incentive, no salary)

**What they provide:**
- Credibility: "If [Respected Name] believes in this, there's something real here"
- Network: access to referral partners, other investors, potential partners
- Strategic input: helps refine model, identify risks
- Investor introductions: warm intros to their networks

**Timing:** Recruit advisors early (Month 1–3) before heavy fundraising

---

## Asset 5: Industry Research / Market Validation

**What:** Third-party validation that the problem and opportunity are real.

**Sources:**
- AARP research on senior living preferences
- Census data on 65+ population growth
- News articles on senior care shortage
- Academic research on aging in place
- Analyst reports (if available) on senior care market
- Interview/survey data from seniors, families, referral partners

**Format:**
- One-pager: "The Case for Senior Housing Infrastructure"
- Data visualization: population trends, supply gaps, market size
- Quote collection: experts and thought leaders on the opportunity

**Timing:** Develop Month 1–2

---

## Asset 6: Proof of Team Execution

**What:** Evidence that your team can actually execute.

**Include:**
- Previous company success stories (if applicable)
- Media mentions, awards, recognitions
- LinkedIn endorsements and profile strength
- Letters of recommendation from previous employers
- Track record of fundraising, team building, or operations

**Format:**
- Team bios page (1 page per person, headshot + background)
- "Meet the team" video (1–2 min)
- Previous company case studies (if applicable)

**Timing:** Have ready before pitch meetings (Month 2–3)

---

## Asset 7: Operator / Landlord Response Letters

**What:** Warm letters from operators or landlords you've approached, expressing interest or commitment.

**What they should say:**
- Problem they face (occupancy, succession, staffing, distress)
- Why they're interested in DFFB's model
- Willingness to work together
- Commitment to partnership (LOI or MOU)

**Format:**
- Formal letter from operator/landlord
- Contact info for investor reference calls
- Signed and dated

**Timing:** Collect as you have conversations; aim for 3–5 by Month 4

---

## Asset 8: Comparable Company Analysis

**What:** Shows that similar businesses have achieved strong valuations/returns.

**Companies to analyze:**
- Brookdale Senior Living (NYSE: BDL) — large operator, public company
- Five Star Senior Living — regional operator
- Cascadian Assisted Living — small-scale model (similar to DFFB)
- Chartwell Retirement Residences (public Canadian company)
- Private senior care roll-ups or acquisitions in past 2–3 years

**Metrics to include:**
- Revenue multiples (3–6x EBITDA typical)
- Margin profiles (15–25% EBITDA margin typical)
- Occupancy levels (85–95% typical at maturity)
- Growth rates (5–15% annually typical)

**Format:**
- Table comparing companies
- One-pager: "Comparable Exits & Valuations"
- Key insight: "If [Comparable] trades at 4x EBITDA and we achieve $3M EBITDA in Year 5, exit valuation is $12M+"

**Timing:** Develop Month 2–3

---

## Asset 9: Financial Projections with Sensitivity Analysis

**What:** Detailed financial model showing multiple scenarios (conservative, base, aggressive).

**Include:**
- Conservative case (slower ramp, lower occupancy, lower margins)
- Base case (mid projections)
- Aggressive case (faster scaling, higher occupancy)
- Sensitivity tables (what if occupancy is 5% lower? Staffing 5% higher?)
- Exit scenarios (acquisition at 4x, 5x, 6x EBITDA; cash flow hold)
- Investor returns in each scenario

**Format:**
- Excel model (detailed)
- Executive summary (one page showing each scenario)
- Waterfall charts (revenue → occupancy → margin → EBITDA)

**Timing:** Develop Month 3–4, update quarterly

**Include:**

## Asset 14: Press Kit / One-Pager Suite

**What:** Collection of short, polished documents for quick reference.

**Include:**
- DFFB company overview (1 page)
- The problem/opportunity (1 page)
- The solution/model (1 page)
- Financial highlights (1 page)
- Team bios (1 page)

- PDF folder with all one-pagers

**Timing:** Develop Month 2–3, update quarterly

## Usage in Investor Meetings

**What to send before meeting:**
- One-pager overview
- Case study (if available)
- Financial model summary

**What to show in meeting:**
- Pitch deck (digital)
- Property photos / tour video
- Operator testimonials (video clips)

**What to leave behind:**
- Printed one-pager suite
- Case study
- Comparable companies analysis
- Team bios

**What to provide after meeting:**
- Full financial model (if interested)
- Compliance documentation
- Reference contact info
- Follow-up materials (no new asks; just resources)

- **Investor credibility score:** How many assets do you have ready? (Aim: 8+ by Month 6)
- **Media coverage:** X articles by Month 6
- **Testimonials:** X landlord/operator endorsements by Month 6
- **Advisor board:** 2–3 strong advisors recruited by Month 3
- **Pilot data:** 2+ properties with 3+ months operational data by Month 4
- **Referral partnerships:** 5+ signed partnerships by Month 5

---

## Pro Tips

1. **Start early:** Don't wait to have "perfect" data. Collect real pilot data and share it, even if messy.
2. **Authenticity over polish:** Real testimonial videos are more powerful than polished PR.
3. **Update frequently:** Refresh metrics and financials monthly; investors respect momentum.
4. **Social proof:** Every testimonial, partnership, and advisor adds credibility. Accumulate fast.
5. **Feedback loop:** Share progress with advisors monthly; incorporate their feedback. Shows you're listening and iterating.
